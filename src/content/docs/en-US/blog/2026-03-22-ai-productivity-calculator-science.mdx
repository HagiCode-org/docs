## I Might Be Replaced by an Agent, So I Ran the Numbers

> Quantifying AI replacement risk with data: An in-depth analysis of how the HagiCode team redefined competitiveness assessment standards for knowledge workers using 6 core formulas.

## Background

In today's era of rapid AI development, every knowledge worker faces an urgent question: **Will I be eliminated in the AI age?**

This question might sound a bit alarmist, but many people are genuinely worried about it. You just master a framework, and AI immediately says your role is about to be replaced; you finally become proficient in a language, only to find that someone using AI is three times as productive—I'm sure you, reading this, can relate to this anxiety to some degree.

Actually, this anxiety isn't entirely unfounded. After all, no one wants to admit that skills they've spent years developing might be surpassed by a ChatGPT. But anxiety aside, life must go on, right?

Traditional approaches often discuss replacement risk from the perspective of "what AI can do," but this method overlooks two key dimensions:

1. **Enterprise perspective**: Whether a company is willing to equip an employee with AI tools depends on the cost-effectiveness of AI costs relative to labor costs. It's not that AI can replace a role, so companies will immediately replace people—they still need to calculate the economic equation. After all, capitalists aren't philanthropists; every penny must be spent where it matters most.

2. **Efficiency perspective**: The efficiency gains from AI need to be quantified, rather than simply assuming "using AI makes you stronger." If your efficiency with AI increases 2-fold, but someone else's increases 5-fold, that's a significant gap. It's like in school—everyone attends the same lectures, but some score 90 while others barely pass—that's how the gap widens.

So the key question is: **How do we transform this vague anxiety into quantifiable metrics?**

After all, knowing where you stand is better than fumbling in the dark. That's what we're discussing today—the design logic behind the AI Productivity Calculator developed by the HagiCode team.

## Core Formulas: 6 Key Metrics

### 1. Annual Total Labor Cost

The true cost a company pays for an employee goes far beyond salary. Many people only discover this when changing jobs—clearly negotiating a 20k monthly salary, why does only 14k arrive? The company pays more than 20k; social insurance, housing fund, training, and recruitment costs all need to be factored in.

According to the implementation in `calculate-ai-risk.ts`:

```
Annual Total Labor Cost = Annual Salary × (1 + City Coefficient) + Annual Salary / 12
```

The city coefficient reflects talent recruitment and retention costs across different cities:

| City Tier | Representative Cities | Coefficient |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | Beijing/Shanghai/Shenzhen/Guangzhou | 0.4 |
| New Tier 1 | Hangzhou/Chengdu/Suzhou/Nanjing | 0.3 |
| Tier 2 | Wuhan/Xi'an/Tianjin/Zhengzhou | 0.2 |
| Other | Yichang/Luoyang, etc. | 0.1 |

The Tier 1 city coefficient is 0.4, meaning companies need to pay approximately 40% additional costs for recruitment, training, social insurance, etc. The comprehensive cost of hiring someone in Beijing is indeed significantly higher than in Tier 2 cities.

After all, surviving in big cities comes with higher living costs—this is another form of "drifter tax," I suppose.

### 2. Comprehensive Token Unit Price

Different AI models have Input and Output prices, with significant differences between them. In code scenarios, the input-output ratio is approximately 3:1—you give AI a piece of code to review, and the output analysis text is usually much shorter than the input code.

Comprehensive unit price calculation formula:

```
Comprehensive Unit Price = (Input-Output Ratio × Input Unit Price + Output Unit Price) / (Input-Output Ratio + 1)
```

Take GPT-5 as an example:
- Input: $2.5/1M tokens
- Output: $15/1M tokens
- Comprehensive = (3 × 2.5 + 15) / 4 = $5.625/1M tokens

For USD-priced models, currency conversion is also required. The HagiCode team set this exchange rate at 7.25, which will be updated with market fluctuations.

Exchange rates are like the stock market—no one can predict them accurately. We just follow along, that's all.

### 3. Annual AI Cost

```
Daily AI Cost = Daily Token Demand (M) × Comprehensive Unit Price (CNY/1M)
Annual AI Cost = Daily AI Cost × 264 working days
```

264 = 22 days/month × 12 months, which is the number of annual working days under a standard work schedule. Why not 365 days? Because you need to account for weekends, holidays, sick leave, and other factors.

After all, we're not robots; when it's time to rest, we need to rest. Although AI might not need rest, we still need to leave ourselves some breathing space.

### 4. Core Innovation: Equivalent Manpower

This is the core of the entire assessment system and the most insightful aspect of the HagiCode team.

```
Affordable Workflow Shares = Annual Total Labor Cost / Annual AI Cost
Affordable Ratio = min(Affordable Workflow Shares, 1)
Equivalent Manpower = 1 + (Efficiency Multiplier - 1) × Affordable Ratio
```

Wait, this formula is a bit convoluted—let me explain:

Traditional approaches would simply say "your efficiency has increased 2-fold," but this formula considers a key constraint: **Is the company's AI budget sustainable?**

For example: Xiao Ming's efficiency increased 3-fold, but his annual AI consumption cost is 300k; while the company's annual salary for him is only 200k. In this case, although Xiao Ming's personal efficiency is high, he's actually unsustainable—the company cannot operate at a loss just to maintain his high efficiency.

The affordable ratio means exactly this: if a company can only afford 0.5 AI workflows, then Xiao Ming's equivalent manpower = 1 + (3-1) × 0.5 = 2 people, not 3 people.

**Core Insight**: It's not about how high your efficiency multiplier is, but whether the company can afford the AI investment required to maintain that efficiency.

Actually, this principle is quite simple, but many people haven't thought about it from this angle. After all, we're used to looking at problems from our own perspective and rarely consider it from the boss's perspective—their money didn't come from the wind either.

### 5. Cost-Benefit Ratio

```
AI Cost Ratio = Annual AI Cost / Annual Total Labor Cost
Efficiency Gain = Efficiency Multiplier - 1
Cost-Benefit Ratio = Efficiency Gain / AI Cost Ratio
```

- Cost-Benefit Ratio < 1: AI investment is not worthwhile, efficiency gains don't justify costs
- Cost-Benefit Ratio 1-2: Just worthwhile
- Cost-Benefit Ratio > 2: High returns, strongly recommended

This metric is particularly useful for enterprise managers to quickly assess whether a role is worth investing in AI tools.

After all, ROI is king—you can claim your efficiency improved all you want, but if costs explode, no one will buy it.

### 6. Risk Level

Risk is categorized based on equivalent manpower:

| Equivalent Manpower | Risk Level | Conclusion |
|---------------------|------------|------------|
| >= 2.0 | High Risk | Once colleagues have the same conditions, they pose a high threat to you |
| 1.5 - 2.0 | Warning | Colleagues have begun to form a significant efficiency advantage |
| < 1.5 | Safe | Can still maintain the gap for now |

Seeing this table, you probably have a rough idea in mind. But don't be too anxious—anxiety doesn't solve problems anyway—better to think about how to improve your efficiency multiplier.

## Practical Usage Example

Assume you're a developer in Beijing with an annual salary of 400k, using Claude Sonnet 4.6, consuming 50M tokens daily, and self-assessing a 3-fold efficiency improvement. Sample input:

```typescript
const input = {
  annualIncomeCny: 400000,
  cityTier: "tier1",           // Beijing
  modelId: "claude-sonnet-4-6",
  performanceMultiplier: 3.0,
  dailyTokenUsageM: 50,
}

// Calculation process
// Annual Total Labor Cost = 400k × (1 + 0.4) + 400k/12 ≈ 603.3k
// Annual AI Cost ≈ 50 × 7.125 × 264 ≈ 94k
// Affordable Workflow Shares ≈ 603.3 / 94 ≈ 6.4 shares
// Equivalent Manpower = 1 + (3 - 1) × 1 = 3 people
```

Conclusion: If your colleagues have the same conditions, they can produce the output of 3 people—you're already in the high-risk zone.

If you find your AI usage is "not worthwhile" (cost-benefit ratio < 1), consider:

1. **Reduce Token Consumption**: Use more efficient prompts, reduce ineffective requests
2. **Choose Cost-Effective Models**: Such as DeepSeek-V3 (priced in CNY, cheaper)
3. **Improve Efficiency Multiplier**: Learn advanced Agent techniques to truly turn AI into productivity

Actually, these problems boil down to an art of balance. Use too much and you waste money; use too little and it's ineffective—finding that sweet spot is key.
